Strategy Briefing • Feb 2026

The 2025–26 Infrastructure Cohort

Executive Takeaway

  • The "App Era" is over. Investment has shifted from fan-facing apps to backend infrastructure: identity, compliance, and automated video logic.
  • AI is now "Agentic". New tools (e.g., PlayVision, Snapp Stats) don't just tag video; they rank players and answer questions. This threatens traditional scouting workflows.
  • Consolidation is the strategy. Ventures like OTTO Sport AI are rolling up fragmented point solutions (ticketing, recruiting, management) into single operating systems.
  • Risk lies in inaction. Organisations clinging to legacy "barcode" ticketing or manual compliance review (NIL) are leaking revenue and facing reputational exposure.

What’s Happening

We are tracking a structural pivot in the 2025–2026 startup vintage. The startups securing capital are not trying to "disrupt" the fan experience with another screen. Instead, they are fixing the unglamorous plumbing of the sports economy.

OTTO Sport AI raised $16.5M not to build a new game, but to unify the messy backend of youth sports. KNOT Technologies is replacing standard ticketing with identity-based access control. DocuLingo is automating legal review for NIL contracts.

This is "Boring Tech"—essential, high-switching-cost infrastructure that dictates how money and data move.

Why It Matters

  • Commercial: Identity-based ticketing (KNOT) stops revenue leakage to the secondary market. If you don't know who is in your venue, you cannot monetise them.
  • Integrity & Governance: Automated compliance (DocuLingo) is the only scalable way to manage thousands of athlete contracts without a massive legal headcount.
  • Performance: AI computer vision (PlayVision) democratises data that used to cost millions. Smaller federations can now access "Premier League" level scouting insights.

Reality Check

Procurement Friction: These systems replace core workflows. Switching costs are high. Expect resistance from internal staff ("we've always done it this way") and long integration timelines with legacy CRMs.

Data Quality: "Agentic AI" (like Otto Pilot) is only as good as the data it accesses. If your federation's historical data is unstructured or siloed, these tools will fail to deliver value on Day 1.

What to Do Next

  • Audit your "Ghost Data": How many fans in your venue are unverified? If it's >20%, pilot an identity-ticketing layer like KNOT.
  • Review Scouting Spend: Are you paying for manual tagging? Test a computer-vision pilot (Snapp Stats/PlayVision) to see if you can reduce headcount or reallocate staff to higher-value analysis.
  • Consolidate Vendors: If you use 5 different tools for youth management (registration, payments, scheduling), look at unified OS solutions (OTTO) to reduce API maintenance costs.

Watch-outs

Vendor Viability: This cohort is young (Seed/Pre-Seed). Do not sign 5-year contracts without escape clauses or code escrow. Many will be acquired or pivot.

"AI Washing": Ensure the product actually uses proprietary models (e.g., Large Visual Models) and isn't just a wrapper around a generic LLM. Ask for a technical demo on your data, not theirs.
